# **Technical Sourcing Evaluation of Turkish Print Finishers: Soft-Touch Lamination (Mat Selefon) and Gold Foil Capabilities**

## **Technical and Linguistic Alignment of Finishing Services in Turkey**

For international procurement professionals, sourcing high-end print embellishments in Turkey requires translating brand specifications into local commercial terms. The standard Turkish industry term for film lamination is *selefon*.1 When a project specifies matte lamination, it is referred to locally as *mat selefon*.3 Soft-touch lamination—a premium packaging finish that provides a velvet-like tactile texture—is executed as a specialized variation of *mat selefon* utilizing specialized oriented polypropylene (OPP) or polyurethane-based matte films.  
Similarly, gold foil printing is cataloged under several names on Turkish trade registries, depending on the application machinery. It is most frequently documented as *gilding* 4, *hot stamping* 5, or *hot/cold foil printing*.4  
From a chemical and physical standpoint, executing gold foil stamping over a matte or soft-touch laminated surface requires precise technical calibration. Sourcing agents must verify that the print finishers maintain proper control over the surface energy of the laminated substrate. Standard OPP matte films typically require a corona treatment to achieve a surface tension of at least ![][image1] (or ![][image2]) to ensure that the thermal adhesive on the gold foil carrier bonds securely during the hot stamping process. If this surface tension is not maintained, the gold foil will experience poor adhesion, leading to flaking or registration errors along fine-line graphics.

## **Upstream Supply Chain Interdependencies and Raw Material Sourcing**

The performance and cost-efficiency of Turkish print finishers are directly tied to a highly localized upstream supply chain that provides raw foils, specialized chemical adhesives, and machinery. This domestic self-sufficiency isolates Turkish converters from many global supply chain disruptions, allowing for competitive pricing and reliable lead times.

### **Raw Material and Foil Manufacturing**

The metallic foils used in gold foil printing are supported by heavy domestic metallurgical industries. For example, Panda Aluminium, located in Ankara, is a major manufacturer of aluminum foil, producing thicknesses ranging from ![][image3] to ![][image4].16 This domestic supply feeds local lamination and foil-converting operations, reducing the reliance on imported foil stocks for laminated packaging structures.16

### **Specialized Adhesives and Chemical Formulations**

The structural integrity of a soft-touch laminate depends entirely on the adhesive used to bond the synthetic film to the paperboard substrate. Without high-performance adhesives, the heat and shear stress of subsequent gold foil stamping would cause localized bubbling or delamination.  
Turkish printers rely on specialized domestic chemical producers like Ş. Barkey Chemical, established in 1981, which manufactures polymer dispersions and synthetic adhesives.17 Barkey Chemical produces specialized hot-melt and acrylic adhesives designed specifically for bonding foil-to-paper and plastic-paper laminates.17  
Furthermore, companies like Purin Polyurethane in Kocaeli specialize in manufacturing high-performance polyurethane (PU) resins and lamination adhesives.18 These chemical formulations provide the high thermal stability required to withstand the high temperatures of gold foil stamping dies without degrading the underlying matte film.

### **Machinery and Equipment Integration**

The domestic print infrastructure is further supported by Turkish machinery builders, which lowers capital expenditure costs for local print shops. Intergrup Plastik, along with its affiliate Plasmek Plastik Sanayi, manufactures and distributes industrial profile bending and lamination machinery.19  
In the printing sector, Mikron Makina Sanayi, based in Tuzla, İstanbul, has been manufacturing flexographic and gravure printing machinery since 2003\.20 This localized machine-building sector allows Turkish finishers to rapidly scale their production capacities and maintain their equipment with minimal downtime.

## **Strategic Procurement and Export Feasibility for International Buyers**

Turkey's position as a major packaging and printing hub for Europe and the Middle East is reinforced by favorable trade agreements and specialized industrial clusters.

### **Regulatory and Tariff Advantages**

For European Union buyers, the most significant trade advantage is Turkey's Customs Union agreement. Using the A.TR movement certificate, Turkish print exporters like Turkish Prints can ship finished paper, cardboard, and laminated packaging materials to EU countries completely customs-free.11 This bypasses the tariff barriers and customs delays associated with sourcing from Asian markets.

### **Geographic and Industrial Clustering**

The Turkish printing and paper industry is highly clustered, creating an ecosystem that fosters rapid turnaround times. Major paperboard producers, such as Türkoğlu Kağıt Karton Sanayi in Hadımköy, İstanbul, supply the raw greyboard and duplex board reels used to manufacture rigid boxes.21 This proximity to primary material suppliers allows finishers in nearby Beylikdüzü (like Ömür Etiket) or Tuzla (like Mikron Makina) to source substrate materials, apply matte laminations, execute gold foil stamping, and ship finished goods within very tight timelines.4

### **Procurement Selection Strategies**

When designing a sourcing matrix for Turkish print finishes, procurement officers must choose between two primary operational models:

* **Integrated Production Model:** Partnering with fully integrated suppliers like Ömür Etiket or Umur Basım minimizes supply chain risk.4 Because the offset printing, film lamination, and gold foil stamping are executed under a single roof, the manufacturer assumes sole responsibility for substrate compatibility and finish adhesion. This model is highly recommended for technically demanding cosmetic and pharmaceutical packaging where registration tolerances are tight and BRC safety compliance is required.4  
* **Unbundled Trade Finisher Model:** For cost-sensitive, high-volume retail packaging, buyers can achieve lower unit costs by sourcing raw offset printing from high-capacity print shops and contracting the lamination directly to specialized trade finishers like Süper Selefon.2 While this unbundled model reduces raw-material margins, it requires the buyer to actively coordinate the logistics between the print shop and the finisher, and increases the risk of quality disputes if delamination or curling occurs post-lamination.
